Root Cause Analysis of Fact-Checking Examiner Failure
01
Limited Coverage — Only 12% of Reports Touched
The SVM classifier scored most sentences — including hallucinated ones — above the P(Real) > 0.5 threshold, so they were kept
Scale
Only 60 / 500 reports modified; 68 sentences removed in total (0.136 / report)
02
Low Precision — ~50% of Removed Sentences Were Correct
Roughly half of the deleted sentences were true positives — real clinical findings mistaken for hallucinations
Case 0318ea6e
Correct pneumothorax（氣胸）finding (confirmed by GT) was flagged as fake and deleted — a false negative that drives CheXBert Macro down as the threshold rises (0.3535 → 0.1826 at 0.8)
03
Root Cause — Downstream Filtering Can't Fix Upstream QA Bias
The Examiner only screens sentences after generation; it cannot correct the QA step's tendency to answer "Yes" under insufficient image evidence
Hallucinations that are vaguely worded or partially consistent with the image evade detection
Without fixing the QA affirmative bias upstream, sentence-level post-processing cannot meaningfully improve correction quality — future work should shift filtering from post-hoc deletion to pre-generation screening
